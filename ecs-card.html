Construction Plant Competence Scheme (CPCS)

ECS Electrotechnical Certification Scheme The Electrotechnical Certification Scheme (ECS) is the official certification system for electrotechnical workers in the UK. It verifies qualifications, experience, and competency in various electrical-related roles. Holding an ECS card proves your skills, identity, and adherence to industry standards.

Why You Need an ECS Card

  • Proof of Competency: Recognized certification in the electrotechnical industry.
  • Ensures Safety: Demonstrates knowledge of health and safety regulations.
  • Verification of Qualifications: Confirms industry-recognized skills and training.
  • Access to Job Sites: Many employers and sites require ECS certification.

How to Apply for an ECS Card

  • Create a MyECS Account: Register and submit an online application.
  • Meet Qualification Requirements: Check the specific requirements for your card type.
  • Pass the Health & Safety Assessment: Complete the ECS Health, Safety & Environmental test.
  • Submit Your Application: Provide necessary documents and pay the required fees.

What is the Health, Safety & Environment Test?

The Health, Safety & Environment Test (also known as the ECS Test) is a check of your basic knowledge of safety measures that must be taken while working on site. There are several tests available covering different occupations and the different rules that must be taken into account while working with certain equipment or on certain work areas.
